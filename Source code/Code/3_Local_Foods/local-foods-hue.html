    The Exquisite Street Food of Hue

    Hue

    Banh Bot Loc: Tapioca Dumplings

    Banh Bot Loc are delightful Vietnamese tapioca dumplings filled with shrimp or pork. These chewy, translucent dumplings are typically steamed and served with a savory dipping sauce. Known for their unique texture and flavor, Banh Bot Loc are often enjoyed as a snack or appetizer, making them a favorite in Vietnamese cuisine.

    Hue

    Banh Nam: Steamed Rice Cakes

    Banh Nam are traditional Vietnamese steamed rice cakes made from a mixture of rice flour and mung bean paste. These cakes are typically wrapped in banana leaves and filled with shrimp or pork. Banh Nam is known for its soft texture and subtle flavors, often served with a side of dipping sauce. They make for a delicious snack or appetizer, showcasing the culinary heritage of Vietnam.

    Hue

    Banh Canh Nam Pho: Thick Noodle Soup

    Banh Canh Nam Pho is a hearty Vietnamese soup featuring thick rice noodles (banh canh) in a savory broth. This dish is typically made with a variety of meats, such as pork or seafood, and is often garnished with fresh herbs and vegetables. The thick noodles have a chewy texture, making Banh Canh Nam Pho a comforting and satisfying meal, popular in many Vietnamese households.

    Hue

    Com Hen

    Com Hen is a traditional Vietnamese dish featuring rice topped with baby clams. Typically garnished with crispy pork skin, peanuts, fresh herbs, and served with a side of clam broth, this dish offers a delicious mix of flavors and textures. It is especially popular in Hue, reflecting the region's rich culinary traditions. Com Hen is enjoyed as a light meal or snack, showcasing the unique taste of local ingredients.

    Hue

    Bun Bo Hue: Spicy Beef Noodle Soup

    Bun Bo Hue is a famous Vietnamese noodle soup from Hue. It features thick rice noodles in a spicy beef broth made with lemongrass and various spices. Topped with tender beef, herbs, and sometimes pork, this dish is known for its bold flavors and fragrant aroma. Bun Bo Hue is a beloved comfort food, enjoyed by many for its unique taste and satisfying warmth.

    Hue

    Nem Lui Hue: Grilled Pork Skewers

    Nem Lui Hue is a popular dish from Hue, featuring grilled pork skewers marinated with herbs and spices. The pork is typically ground and molded onto bamboo sticks, then grilled over charcoal for a smoky flavor. Served with rice paper, fresh vegetables, and a tasty dipping sauce, Nem Lui Hue offers a delightful combination of flavors and textures. This dish is often enjoyed as a street food favorite and reflects the vibrant culinary culture of Hue.

    Hue

    Che Hue

    Che Hue refers to a variety of traditional sweet desserts from Hue, known for their unique flavors and colorful presentations. These desserts often feature ingredients like mung beans, sticky rice, coconut milk, and various fruits. Common types include Che Ba Mien (three-colored dessert) and Che Bap (sweet corn pudding). Che Hue is typically served chilled or warm and is enjoyed as a refreshing treat, showcasing the rich culinary heritage of the region.

    Hue

    Banh Beo: Steamed Rice Cakes

    Banh Beo are small, steamed rice cakes popular in Vietnam. Made from rice flour, they are typically topped with minced shrimp, scallions, and crispy pork skin. Served with a savory dipping sauce, Banh Beo offers a light and flavorful snack or appetizer.
