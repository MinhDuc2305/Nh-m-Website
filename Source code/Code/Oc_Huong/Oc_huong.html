    Ốc hương, a prized sea snail in Vietnamese cuisine, is known for its tender meat and natural sweetness, making it a popular seafood delicacy

    The name ‘ốc hương’ translates to ‘snail with a fragrant aroma,’ a fitting description of the enticing scent it releases when cooked. This delicacy is prepared in a variety of ways, each showcasing the unique flavor and texture of the snail’s meat.

    One of the most popular methods is stir-frying ốc hương with garlic and chili, where the snails are cooked until perfectly tender and infused with a fragrant, spicy sauce that highlights their natural sweetness. Another common preparation is steaming the snails with lemongrass, allowing their delicate flavors to shine through without overpowering them. Dipping sauces, like a tangy salt, pepper, and lime mix or a rich, creamy coconut sauce, often accompany the dish, adding layers of flavor to every bite

    Ốc hương is typically enjoyed in Vietnamese seafood restaurants or at bustling street food stalls, where diners use toothpicks to extract the tender meat from the shell. Each bite offers a delightful combination of savory, sweet, and aromatic elements. This dish is a must-try for seafood lovers, embodying the freshness and bold flavors of Vietnamese coastal cuisine. Whether stir-fried, steamed, or grilled, ốc hương brings a taste of the sea to the table, making it a beloved choice among locals and travelers alike.
